About Me

As a recently minted PhD student at IIT Kanpur's Department of Materials Science and Engineering, I work on modeling of multiphase turbulent fluid flow and interfacial mass-transfer. I've worked extensive with OpenFOAM with in-depth configuration of Volume-of-Fluid (VOF) and lagrangian particle tracking (DPM) methods. I developed a customized multiphase solver with modular capabilities to harness algebraic and geometric VOF methods. Additionally, I am currently working on stable multi-species transport within domains with immiscible phases, and mass-transfer at the interfaces of these phases. Before my PhD, I briefly worked in Data based consulting with Finance and Steelmaking firms, where I worked on computer vision to digitize documents, as well as on data analytics to draw correlations between process parameters to reveal key-drivers.
